When the goods arrive, our first step is to check the moisture content, which should ideally range between 28°C to 30°C without any additional heating. To prepare the wood for planning, we place it under sunlight for a minimum of 10 to 12 hours to ensure a superior finishing result. Utilizing a Thickness Planner machine, we meticulously plane the goods to precision. Once the wood is ready, it is placed on the cutter machine, where we set the desired length. During this process, we periodically verify the length, ensuring accuracy. Subsequently, the planks are handed over to our skilled carpenters who craft pallets tailored to the specified dimensions. These pallets are then stored in a designated area without any heating. Once the pallets are ready for the next phase, we move them to our heat treatment plant, maintaining a constant temperature of 80°C for a duration of four hours. During this period, we closely monitor the temperature, ensuring that the moisture content remains within the range of 12°C to 15°C. Once we confirm compliance with these conditions, the pallets are placed in the heat treatment area, effectively rendering them antifungal. Finally, we prepare the pallets for distribution by grading them and affixing the (IPPC) heat symbol along with the corresponding date, adhering to our company's standards
